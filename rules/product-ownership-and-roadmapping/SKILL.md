# Product Ownership and Roadmapping

## Principles
- Outcomes over output; problems before solutions; learning loops over certainty.
- Focus: few bets with clear hypotheses and success metrics.
- Transparent trade-offs; align stakeholders early and often.

## Instructions

### Step 1: Vision → Strategy → Outcomes
- Articulate product vision and strategy (target customer, problem space, differentiation).
- Translate to 1–3 outcome-based OKRs per quarter.

### Step 2: Discovery and problem framing
- Map Jobs To Be Done (JTBD) and user journeys; collect pains and unmet needs.
- Synthesize insights into problem statements with evidence (quotes, data).

### Step 3: Opportunity backlog
- List opportunities with: evidence, target user, JTBD, impact hypothesis, risks.
- Distinguish opportunities (problems) from solutions (ideas).

### Step 4: Prioritization
- Score with RICE (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort) or ICE; document assumptions.
- Consider strategic bets (longer-term) vs quick wins; maintain a balanced portfolio.

### Step 5: Roadmap (Now / Next / Later)
- Now: committed, sized, staffed. Next: likely, discovery in progress. Later: options.
- Avoid date-driven promises beyond Now unless contractual; show themes and outcomes.

### Step 6: PRDs (problem-first)
- PRD sections: Context, Problem, Goals/Non-goals, Users & Scenarios, Constraints, Metrics, Risks.
- Acceptance criteria with examples; rollout + success measurement plan.

### Step 7: MVP slicing and sequencing
- Decompose by value slices (earliest learning/impact) not components; reduce scope until testable.
- Sequence with dependencies; prepare kill/iterate criteria.

### Step 8: Go-to-market and launch
- Audience, proposition, channels, pricing/packaging (if applicable).
- Content checklist: docs, announcements, onboarding, support enablement.
- Rollout plan: beta → limited GA → full GA with flags and rollback steps.

### Step 9: Measure and learn
- Define North Star metric tree; instrument events; build dashboards.
- Post-launch review at 1, 4, 12 weeks; decide ship/iterate/retire.

## Checklists

### Roadmap quality
- Problems and outcomes clear; avoids feature laundry list.
- Now items staffed and sized; risks visible; dependencies mapped.
- Themes tie to OKRs; stakeholder alignment captured.

### PRD quality
- Problem and success metrics explicit; acceptance criteria testable.
- Constraints, risks, and assumptions documented; privacy/security noted.
- Rollout + measurement plan included.

### Launch readiness
- Docs, onboarding, support, billing/legal (if needed) prepared.
- Telemetry and dashboards live; incident plan and rollback ready.

## Patterns
- Opportunity Solution Tree to connect discovery → decisions → delivery.
- Now/Next/Later roadmap for clarity without fake certainty.
- North Star Framework with input metrics for team focus.

## Troubleshooting
- Build trap (shipping features without impact): return to problems/OKRs.
- Misalignment: run stakeholder mapping and pre-reads; decide trade-offs explicitly.
- Overcommitting: cap WIP; maintain a clear “Not Now” list with rationale.
